# Research

My research interests lie at the intersection of **Computer Systems & Architecture** and **Machine Learning**, with a strong focus on developing **sustainable, efficient, and intelligent computing solutions**. I am particularly passionate about addressing the challenges of deploying complex ML models in resource-constrained environments, from tiny energy-harvesting sensors to powerful edge servers.

My work aims to make AI more accessible, reliable, and environmentally friendly. Below are some of the key areas I focus on:

## Deep Learning at the Edge

This area explores novel architectures and software techniques to enable efficient execution of deep neural networks directly on edge devices. This reduces latency, enhances privacy, and minimizes data transmission costs. My work includes developing specialized hardware accelerators, model compression techniques (quantization, pruning), and resource-aware scheduling algorithms tailored for edge platforms.

## Continuous Learning Systems & Resource-Aware ML

Traditional ML models are often static after deployment. Continuous learning aims to enable systems to adapt and improve over time as new data becomes available, without requiring full retraining from scratch. I investigate methods for on-device learning, incremental model updates, and efficient knowledge transfer in dynamic environments, all while being mindful of the limited computational and energy resources available at the edge.

## Intermittent Computing & Non-volatile Processors

Many emerging edge devices, particularly those powered by energy harvesting, operate intermittently due to fluctuating power availability. My research in this domain focuses on designing resilient hardware (e.g., non-volatile processors) and software systems that can make forward progress, maintain data integrity, and ensure computational correctness despite frequent power failures. This is crucial for enabling long-term autonomous operation of batteryless sensors and IoT devices.

## Sustainable Computing & Energy Harvesting Wireless Sensor Networks

Sustainability is a critical concern for the future of computing. I work on developing ultra-low-power systems that can operate solely on harvested ambient energy (e.g., solar, kinetic, RF). This involves co-designing energy-efficient hardware, power management strategies, and communication protocols for wireless sensor networks, enabling them to perform complex tasks like on-device intelligence for human activity recognition or environmental monitoring for extended periods without battery replacements.

## Computational Storage

As data volumes grow exponentially, moving data between storage and compute units becomes a major bottleneck. Computational storage aims to alleviate this by integrating processing capabilities directly within storage devices. My research explores how to leverage computational storage to accelerate data-intensive applications, particularly for ML workloads, by performing operations like data filtering, pre-processing, and even parts of model inference closer to where the data resides.

## Optimizing XR (VR/AR) Compute Pipelines

Extended Reality (XR) applications, including Virtual Reality (VR) and Augmented Reality (AR), demand high computational power and low latency for an immersive user experience. I investigate methods to optimize the entire XR compute pipeline, from sensor data processing and 3D rendering to AI-driven interactions, often targeting mobile and edge platforms with strict power and thermal constraints.

## Efficient Deep Learning Training & Inference Serving in the Cloud

While edge computing is crucial, the cloud remains vital for training large-scale ML models and serving complex inference requests. My work also extends to optimizing these cloud-based ML workloads, focusing on resource heterogeneity, model serving efficiency (e.g., using ensemble learning and multi-dimensional optimization), and adaptive container provisioning for dynamic deep learning workflows.
