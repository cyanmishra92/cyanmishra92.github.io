I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at The Pennsylvania State University, advised by Dr. Mahmut Taylan Kandemir and Dr. Jack Sampson. My research focuses on **Computer Systems and Architecture for Sustainable Machine Learning**, with a particular emphasis on architecting energy-efficient and continuous learning systems, intelligent energy harvesting wireless sensor networks, and optimizing XR (VR/AR) compute pipelines. I also explore efficient Deep Learning training and inference serving in public cloud environments.

My expertise lies in **hardware/software co-design**, where I specialize in optimizing Machine Learning workloads across heterogeneous compute platforms. This includes accelerator architecture, kernel optimization, and performance modeling for resource-constrained environments. I have a proven track record of designing and implementing energy-efficient systems for large-scale ML deployment, focusing on model compression, microarchitectural innovations, and computational efficiency.

Previously, I worked as a Design Engineer at Intel, focusing on Hardware Accelerators for Machine Learning and Computer Vision. I am passionate about bridging cutting-edge research with impactful industry solutions and am currently on the job market for both academic and industry research opportunities.

My core research interests include:
*   Deep Learning at the Edge
*   Continuous Learning Systems & Resource-Aware ML
*   Intermittent Computing & Non-volatile Processors
*   Sustainable Computing & Energy Harvesting Wireless Sensor Networks
*   Computational Storage
*   Hardware Accelerators for ML/CV
*   Optimizing XR (VR/AR) Compute Pipelines
*   Efficient Deep Learning Training & Inference Serving in the Cloud

Feel free to explore my [publications](/publications/), [projects](/projects/), and [resume](/resume/) for more details on my work. You can reach me via email for collaborations or inquiries.
